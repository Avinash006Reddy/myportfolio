About Me

I'm an aerospace engineering undergraduate at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, specialising in computational fluid dynamics and high-speed aerodynamics. My work spans 2D/3D CFD simulation, structural analysis, and propulsion modelling.

Research Projects

01
Ongoing Jan – Apr 2026
Aerodynamic Performance of 2D Rigid Airfoil Sail for Wind-Assisted Ship Propulsion
  • Conducting 2D steady-state CFD simulations to evaluate aerodynamic performance of a rigid wing-sail under varying apparent wind angles.
  • Investigating lift-to-drag characteristics for thrust generation in auxiliary marine propulsion.
  • Comparing turbulence models (k–ω SST and standard k–ε) to analyze sensitivity of aerodynamic coefficient prediction.
  • Applying mesh refinement near leading-edge and separation regions for improved boundary-layer resolution.
  • Validating CFD results with XFLR5 analysis for lift coefficient trend consistency.
  • Studying Reynolds number regimes representative of realistic marine operating conditions.
ANSYS Fluent k–ω SST k–ε XFLR5 Marine Propulsion
02
Completed Sep – Nov 2025
Analysis of Supersonic Flow over a 2D Diamond Airfoil
  • Modelled symmetric double-wedge airfoil geometry with half edge angle of 10° for compressible flow analysis using CATIA.
  • Performed 2D computational simulations for Mach 1.5–3.0 using density-based solver and k–ω SST turbulence model.
  • Analyzed oblique shock and expansion fan formation, shock-angle variation, and pressure coefficient distribution.
  • Evaluated lift and wave-drag trends across 0°–10° angle of attack.
  • Compared numerical results with classical shock-expansion theory.
ANSYS Fluent Mach 1.5–3.0 Oblique Shocks CATIA V5 Compressible Flow
03
Completed May 2025
Aerodynamic Analysis of a Race Car Rear Spoiler
  • Designed a 3D CAD model of a rear spoiler using CATIA V5 based on automotive aerodynamic design considerations.
  • Conducted aerodynamic evaluation of NACA 5-series rear spoiler at Reynolds number ~3×10⁶ using ANSYS Discovery.
  • Achieved maximum CL = 0.35 at 15° AoA, with peak aerodynamic efficiency (CL/CD ≈ 3.1) at 11° AoA.
  • Investigated flow separation patterns and pressure distribution across varying angles of attack.
  • Evaluated lift (downforce) and drag characteristics; analysed wake structure and aerodynamic efficiency.
ANSYS Discovery CATIA V5 NACA 5-Series Re ~3×10⁶ Downforce
04
Completed Oct – Nov 2025
Structural Behaviour of a Thin-Walled Circular Section
  • Fabricated a 6-layer GFRP thin-walled circular tube (L = 0.75 m, t = 4 mm) using hand layup process for structural testing.
  • Predicted theoretical bending deflection of 0.0806 mm under 50 N; validated experimentally within 18% deviation.
  • Experimentally evaluated stress distribution and deformation under applied loading conditions.
  • Computed torsional response (T = 5 Nm) with angular twist of ~0.06° using ANSYS Static Structural.
  • Compared stress and deformation trends between numerical and experimental approaches.
